# Asset Management Policy

## Overview

This policy ensures that all information and technology assets at YOTTABYTE are properly managed, maintained, and protected to prevent security risks and ensure data protection.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to ensure appropriate security controls are applied to all information assets and that they are managed in a secure and organized manner.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees
- Contractors and third parties
- All systems, processes, and information assets within YOTTABYTE

---

## Asset Lifecycle

- All asset purchases must follow the company procurement process
- IT assets must be approved and acquired through the IT Department
- Applies to all assets, including free or low-cost items
- Assets must be securely configured
- Assets must be returned upon termination
- Disposed assets must be securely wiped to prevent data recovery

---

## Asset Inventory

- All assets must be:
  - Identified
  - Inventoried
  - Maintained
  - Documented
- Includes both on-premises and cloud assets
- Each asset must have an owner responsible for its security
- Updates to the asset register must be reviewed
- Inventory includes:
  - Hardware
  - Software (including SaaS)
  - Databases
  - Code repositories

---

## Asset Register

The asset register must include:

- Asset name
- Asset type (hard copy / digital)
- Storage or location
- Asset owner
- Confidentiality, integrity, and availability level
- Asset value (high, medium, low)
- Classification level

---

## Data Classification

All data must be classified into:

### Restricted
- Highly sensitive
- Known to limited individuals
- Disclosure can severely harm the organization

### Confidential
- Requires strong protection (e.g., encryption)
- Shared only with authorized users

### Internal Use
- Used for normal business operations
- Not for public access

### Public
- Information available for public consumption

### Rules

- Assets must be labeled according to classification
- Labels must be clearly visible
- Unlabeled data must be treated as **Restricted**

---

## Removable Media Handling

- Must follow data classification rules
- Must be protected from:
  - Unauthorized access
  - Misuse
  - Tampering
- Must be securely disposed of
- Must include logical protections (e.g., encryption)

---

## Acceptable Use of Assets

- Users must follow the Acceptable Use Policy when using company assets

---

## Return of Assets

- All assets must be returned upon:
  - Resignation
  - Termination
  - Contract completion
- HR ensures asset return before final settlement
- Missing assets must be reported as incidents
- Unauthorized copying during notice period must be prevented

---

## Labelling of Information

- All assets must be labeled physically or digitally
- Labels must reflect classification level
- All pages of documents must include classification labels
- Physical assets must have unique identification labels

### Classification Labels

- Public
- Internal Use
- Confidential
- Restricted (Need-to-know basis)

### Sensitivity Criteria

- **Confidentiality** → Impact if data is exposed  
- **Integrity** → Impact if data is altered  
- **Availability** → Impact if data is unavailable  

---

## Annual Review

- Policy is reviewed annually or when required
- Internal audits are conducted yearly
- Processes are updated for compliance
