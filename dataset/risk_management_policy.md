## 1. PURPOSE
The purpose of this document is to conduct the risk assessment of YOTTABYTE for information security and privacy based on issues, interested party requirements, dependencies, processes, information, information assets, systems, facilities, people, and processes within the ISMS scope.

---

## 2. RISK MANAGEMENT PROCESS

The risk management process involves systematic application of policies, procedures, and practices to:
- Communication and consultation  
- Context establishment  
- Risk assessment  
- Risk treatment  
- Monitoring and review  

This process is iterative and integrated into organizational decision-making.

---

### 2.1 Communicate and Consult
- Ensure stakeholders understand risks and decisions  
- Promote awareness and gather feedback  
- Managed by Information Security Forum (ISF)  
- Includes reporting, risk register reviews, and coordination  

---

### 2.2 Scope, Context and Criteria
- Scope: ISMS  
- Identify processes, services, and required assets  

---

#### 2.2.1 Identify Information Assets
- Identify all assets within ISMS scope  
- Record in Information Asset Register  

---

#### 2.2.2 Asset Valuation

Assets are evaluated based on:
- Confidentiality (C)  
- Integrity (I)  
- Availability (A)  

**Rating Scale:**
- 1 = Low  
- 2 = Medium  
- 3 = High  

**Formula:**
```
Asset Value = C + I + A
```

---

### Asset Value Classification

| Value | Priority |
|------|--------|
| 9 | Significant (Top Priority) |
| 7–8 | High |
| 5–6 | Medium |
| 3–4 | Low |

---

### 2.3 Identify Risks
Includes:
- Threats and vulnerabilities  
- Affected assets/processes  
- Existing controls  
- Potential impacts  

Methods:
- Self-assessments  
- Vulnerability scans  
- Document reviews  

---

### 2.4 Analyze Risks

Risk = Impact + Likelihood  

---

#### 2.4.1 Impact Levels

| Level | Description |
|------|------------|
| 5 | Extreme |
| 4 | High |
| 3 | Moderate |
| 2 | Minor |
| 1 | Negligible |

---

#### 2.4.2 Likelihood Levels

| Level | Description |
|------|------------|
| 5 | Likely |
| 4 | Possible |
| 3 | Unlikely |
| 2 | Rare |
| 1 | Extremely Rare |

---

### Risk Matrix

| Likelihood \ Impact | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
|-------------------|---|---|---|---|---|
| 5 Likely | Medium | High | High | Extreme | Extreme |
| 4 Possible | Medium | Medium | High | High | Extreme |
| 3 Unlikely | Low | Medium | Medium | High | High |
| 2 Rare | Low | Low | Medium | Medium | High |
| 1 Extremely Rare | Low | Low | Low | Medium | Medium |

---

### Risk Levels

| Level | Action |
|------|-------|
| Extreme | Immediate action (CEO approval) |
| High | Resolve within 3 months |
| Medium | Resolve within 9 months |
| Low | Acceptable risk |

---

### 2.5 Evaluate Risks
- Determine which risks require treatment  
- Consider legal and contractual obligations  

---

### 2.5.1 Treat Risks

| Type | Description |
|------|------------|
| Avoidance | Eliminate risk |
| Mitigation | Reduce likelihood/impact |
| Transfer | Shift to third party |
| Acceptance | Accept risk |

---

### 2.6 Residual Risks
- Remaining risk after treatment  
- Must fall within acceptable risk appetite  

---

### 2.7 Monitor Risks
- Continuous monitoring of risks and controls  
- Focus on high/critical risks  
- Regular reviews and updates  

---

## 3. AUTHORITIES & RESPONSIBILITIES

### 3.1 Risk Owners
- Own and manage risks  
- Monitor effectiveness  
- Ensure accuracy of risk data  
- Implement mitigation strategies  
- Report and communicate risks
