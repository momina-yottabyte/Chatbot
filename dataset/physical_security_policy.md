## 1. PURPOSE

The purpose of this policy is to outline the security requirements of physical areas and equipment at YOTTABYTE.

---

## 2. SCOPE

This policy applies to all systems, people, and processes that constitute the YOTTABYTE information systems, including all employees and other third parties who have access to YOTTABYTE’s information systems.

---

## 3. POLICY STATEMENTS

YOTTABYTE is committed to ensuring the safety of its employees, contractors, and assets and takes the issue of physical security very seriously.

### 3.1 Secure Areas

- YOTTABYTE shall periodically conduct risk assessments to determine appropriate physical security controls, including:
  - Window and door locks  
  - Access control mechanisms  
  - CCTV cameras  
  - Staffed reception areas  
  - Protection against fire, flood, vandalism  

- Staff must challenge individuals not wearing ID cards.
- Access tools (badges, keys, codes) must not be shared.
- Visitors must:
  - Sign in/out  
  - Wear identification badges  

- Keys to secure areas must be centrally managed.
- Cardholder data must be physically secured.
- Upon termination or breach:
  - Access tools must be recovered  
  - Access codes must be changed  

---

### 3.2 Paper and Equipment Security

- Paper-based information must:
  - Have an assigned owner  
  - Be classified appropriately  

- Sensitive documents must be secured using:
  - Locked cabinets  
  - Safes  
  - Secure areas  

- Business-critical systems must use **UPS**.
- Asset inventories must be maintained and updated.
- Data cables must be protected from damage or interception.
- Proper cabling security must be implemented.

---

### 3.3 Equipment Lifecycle Management

- IT assets must be maintained per manufacturer guidelines.
- Equipment service history must be recorded.
- Before disposal or reuse:
  - Data must be securely erased  

- Professional data-wiping tools must be used when required.
- Equipment deliveries must:
  - Be signed by authorized personnel  
  - Be verified against delivery records  

---

### 3.4 Security of Equipment Taken Off-premises

- Equipment may be used off-site for remote work.
- Devices must not be left unattended in public.
- Off-site security controls must be risk-based.
- Transfer of equipment must:
  - Maintain chain-of-custody logs  

---

## 4. ANNUAL REVIEW

- ISMS documents must be reviewed annually or upon updates.
- Internal audits must be conducted at least once per year.
- Reports must be submitted to the **Information Security Forum (ISF)**.
- All procedures must be reviewed for compliance and improvement.
