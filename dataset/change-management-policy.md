## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to provide guidelines for changes made to YOTTABYTE business processes and information technology, including hardware, software, and data.

The intent of the change management policy is to ensure that all changes are:
- Controlled  
- Properly prioritized and scheduled  
- Approved  
- Tested before implementation  

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all systems, people, and processes that constitute YOTTABYTE’s information systems, including employees and third parties.

---

## 3. Policy Statements

YOTTABYTE shall implement a Change Management Process to control changes by:

- Identifying and recording all significant changes  
- Submitting all changes through a **Change Request Form (CRF)**  
- Obtaining management approval via `secops@yottabyte.ltd`  
- Assessing impact, including **security impact**  
- Conducting **risk assessments** and defining mitigation plans  
- Planning and testing changes with fallback procedures  
- Communicating change details to relevant personnel  
- Evaluating whether changes were executed as intended  
- Ensuring changes align with defined scope  
- Conducting management reviews every six months  

---

## 3.1 Change Management Process

The process includes:

### Initiation

Changes may occur due to:
- New module development  
- New product procurement  
- IT infrastructure upgrades  
- Business process or policy changes  
- Changes in ISMS  

---

### Logging & Documentation

- All changes must be logged using a **CRF**
- Proper documentation of:
  - Change details  
  - Attributes  
  - Descriptions  

---

### Review & Approval

- Changes must be reviewed by department managers  
- Security requirements must be evaluated (risk-based)  
- Final approval by management  

---

### Implementation

- Approved changes are scheduled based on priority  
- Only **authorized personnel** can deploy to production  

---

### Post-Implementation

- Documentation and procedures must be updated  
- All activities must be recorded in ticketing systems  
- A **post-implementation review** must be conducted  
- Ensure no adverse impact on systems  

---

### Rollback Planning

- All critical changes must include a **rollback plan**  
- Ensures recovery in case of failure  

---

## 4. Annual Review

- ISMS documents must be reviewed annually or upon updates  
- Internal audits must be conducted annually  
- Reports submitted to the Information Security Forum (ISF)
