## 1. Purpose

This Policy sets out the general principles and processes for the creation and revision of Business Continuity and Recovery plans for YOTTABYTE to counteract interruptions to business activities and to protect critical business processes from the effects of major failures of information systems or disasters and to ensure their timely resumption.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all systems, people and processes that constitute the YOTTABYTE information systems, including all employees and other third parties who have access to YOTTABYTE’s information systems.

---

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Information Security Continuity

- YOTTABYTE shall implement a Business Continuity Plan (BCP) to reduce the impact of potential operational failures and disruption caused by disasters, attacks, and security failures.
- Critical processes and risks shall be identified and prioritized using Business Impact Analysis (BIA).
- Mitigation plans shall be developed and tested.
- Roles and responsibilities must be clearly defined.
- BCP must be integrated into daily operations.
- Safety of human life is the top priority.
- Information security requirements must be included in BCP/DRP.

---

### 3.2 Planning Information Security Continuity

YOTTABYTE shall:

- Identify all assets involved in critical processes.
- Understand:
  - Business objectives of systems
  - Impact of disruptions
  - Risks and priorities
- Recognize need for handling both minor and major incidents.
- Consider insurance as part of risk management.
- Implement preventive and mitigating controls.
- Define requirements for:
  - Confidentiality
  - Integrity
  - Availability
- Allocate sufficient resources.
- Document continuity plans.
- Regularly test and update plans.

---

### 3.3 Business Impact Analysis (BIA)

- Identify events causing disruption (e.g., fire, system failure).
- Assess impact of disruptions.
- Involve business owners in analysis.
- Define recovery requirements based on BIA results.

---

### 3.4 Implementing Information Security Continuity

Key elements:

- Understand risks and their impact.
- Develop continuity strategy aligned with business goals.
- Document plans.

BCP must include:

- **Emergency procedures**
- **Fallback procedures**
- **Resumption procedures**
- **Security controls**

Also includes:

- Awareness and training
- Defined responsibilities
- Maintenance and testing schedule

---

### 3.5 Evaluate and Review Business Continuity

- Plans must be tested regularly.
- Annual tabletop exercises required.
- Test results must be documented.
- Annual review of:
  - BCP
  - BIA

Plans must be updated based on:

- Business changes
- Risk assessments
- Technology changes
- Organizational changes

---

## 4. Annual Review

ISMS documents shall be reviewed annually or upon updates.

Internal audits must be conducted annually, and reports submitted to the Information Security Forum (ISF).
