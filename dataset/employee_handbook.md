# INTRODUCTION

#  Introductory Statement

This handbook is designed to acquaint you with YOTTABYTE and provide you with information about personnel policies, procedures and practices affecting your employment. The manual applies to everyone, including permanent and contractual employees as well as consultants working on specific project(s). You should read, understand, and comply with all the provisions of handbook.

The employee handbook by no means an exhaustive guide and shall be read in conjunction with the employment/ consultancy contract being issued to you at the time of your appointment.

# Changes in Handbook

It may be necessary to change these policies from time to time as our business evolves and grows. Only the Human Resources department in consultation with the management can make amendments in the handbook. Employees will be notified of any changes to the handbook through standard communication channels.

# TALENT MANAGEMENT

> We are committed towards the principles of fair employment and elimination of all discriminatory practices. YOTTABYTE extend equal opportunity to all individuals without regard to gender, race, religion, marital status, political affiliation, age, disability and any other status protected under applicable laws.

# Philosophy

YOTTABYTE recognizes that its employees are a vital component of its success and future growth, and that the quality of its talent is directly proportional to future development and profits. The Talent Acquisition process is central to the organization, and aims to assist the Company in attracting, appointing, and retaining employees with the necessary knowledge, skills and attributes to fulfil the Company's strategic plans and support its values.

# Principles

YOTTABYTE Talent Acquisition Policy has been established to ensure that the company can attract the best available talent, and that any decisions taken to employ an individual comply with the following conditions:

-   Are consistent, fair and merit based

-   Meet business planning needs and strategic objectives

-   Maximize the effectiveness of the company's recruitment and selection practices

-   The Hiring Manager is directly involved and accountable for all resourcing decisions along with Department Head and Talent acquisition department

-   Both Talent Acquisition and the Hiring Manager will ensure that all required documentation and approvals are completed when undertaking recruitment and selection activities

# Hiring Process

## Job Requisition & Job Description

Technical and non-technical hiring requirements are shared with the talent acquisition department by the Hiring Manager or the Department head. When the hiring need arises, the Hiring Manager OR Director(s) must raise a Requisition form stating the following details;

-   Position Title(s)

-   Department

-   Total No. of Position(s)

-   Type of Position

-   Position Description

    -   Responsibilities

    -   Qualification

    -   Skill-set

## Job Posting & Advertisement

Sourcing Mediums includes but are not restricted to;

a.  Career Page

b.  LinkedIn

c.  Facebook

d.  Instagram

e.  Indeed

f.  Referrals

g.  Others

## Resume Screening

Talent Acquisition department will review resumes for minimum experience and qualification and conduct HR screening call with the potential candidates. During the initial screening, the Talent Acquisition team must inquire about candidate's;

-   Total experience and expertise/ skill-set

-   Personality/ culture fit

-   Communication skills

-   Current and expected salary + benefits

-   Notice period

-   Flexibility to work in a certain shift

-   Financial liabilities

-   Reference/ Immediate Relation at Yottabyte (E.g., Spouse, Siblings etc.)

An Employee whose employment has been terminated, for any reason whatsoever, or who is convicted of a criminal offence involving moral turpitude by a court of law, shall not be re-employed by the Company unless decided otherwise by the management, CEO.

## Interviewing

The purpose of an interview(s) is to provide and obtain information that will assist in deciding about a candidate's suitability.

Whilst each interviewer will develop their individual interviewing styles, there are a number of essential characteristics of an interview that must be adhered.

[Guidelines for Interviewer(s):]

-   Review the candidate's resume before commencing the interview. This will allow you to feel more comfortable when the candidate arrives.

-   Always greet and break the ice with the candidate(s).

-   Gauge candidate's technical capability relevant to the position by asking knowledge based, situational, practical questions.

-   Thank the candidate for coming to the interview towards the end.

-   Provide interview feedback to the recruiter within 24 hours of the interview.

-   Hiring Managers/ Interviewers must NOT inquire about the compensation (current and expected) of a candidate at any stage in the hiring process.

## Ability and Psychometric Test

If the position requires assessing candidate's ability and/or personality, the recruiter will ensure provision of test. This will be approved by the Director and Head of HR.

## Selection & Offer

The selection of candidate for hiring must be mutually agreed by both the hiring manager and the recruiter. Offer extension is the final step of the hiring process. Talent Acquisition will send the offer to any potential candidate via email with definite validity.

## Background Checks

Background checks are required to be conducted for the critical positions as identified by the department head alongside HR department. These checks will be conducted prior to the employment of candidate. The type of information that may be collected as part of a background check is based on the duties assigned at the position and requirements of the position.

Background checks will include a national identity card verification, reference check, education and prior employment verification.

i.  **[NIC Verification:]** Validate the candidate\'s NIC number and date of birth.

ii. **[Last Employment Verification/ Reference Checks:]** Verification of employment with the last organization, including dates of employment, position held, compensation, overall conduct, performance rating and reason for departure.

iii. **[Education Verification:]** Check the candidate\'s last qualification; institution, years attended and the degree received. Also, verify the certification(s) claimed.

iv. **[Police Verification:]** A police certificate must be provided by the prospective employee except the C-level executives.

v.  **[Licensing Check/ Driving History:]** Investigation on an individual\'s driving history. This search will be run when driving is an essential requirement of the position.

vi. Copy of education certificate(s)

vii. Copy of experience certificate(s)

viii. Copy of certifications (if any)

ix. Digital photograph

x.  Last salary certificate

xi. Resignation Acceptance from last organization

-   An Interim Performance Review will be conducted by the Line Manager and HR department post completion of 45 days.

-   Additionally, towards the end of probation -- after 90 days, HR department will initiate the confirmation process.

# Guidelines

-   It is the responsibility of the Line Manager/ Head of Department to ensure timely submission of the filled confirmation review form, along with their decision, to HR for further processing.

-   Based on the assessment and decision of the Line Manager and Head of Department, HR will issue a Confirmation Letter or Probation Extension Letter to the employee.

# PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT

Performance evaluation at YOTTABYTE is agile and done throughout the year in the form of weekly check-ins, quarterly assessments, semi-annual, and annual performance reviews. The annual increment cycle runs from July to June every year.

The Company has tailored assessment criteria that have two sections, each for Hard-skills and Soft-skills. It is upon discretion of the Line Manager, Department Head and the HR to decide the weightage of each category i.e., Hard/ Soft Skills.

## Rating Scale Key

-   E(5) Exceptional consistently exceeds job requirements of the position

-   H(4) Highly satisfactory occasionally exceeds job requirements of the position

-   S(3) Satisfactory consistently meets job requirements; performance is fully acceptable

-   N(2) Needs improvement performs at minimum level; significant improvement necessary

-   U(1) Unsatisfactory employee does not perform at an acceptable level

# Increment Effective Date

The company may grant annual increments effective July 1^st^ of each year on the basis of performance, market salary levels and/or company\'s financial position. Only Employees, who have completed eight months of total service, are eligible for an increment unless agreed otherwise upon at the time of joining. All increments are within the absolute discretion of the Company.

# Promotion

When Employees are considered suitable by the Company to fill vacancies in higher positions, the present Employees shall be given prior consideration. Promotions take place during the annual performance appraisal process. Promotion shall be based on education level, overall experience, attitude towards work and abilities to fit in a higher-level position.

Respective Managers shall nominate suitable Employees to available positions before beginning of the appraisal process. All nominations for promotion will be evaluated and decided by the management. All promotions are based on the performance assessment result of the Employee. The managements' decision in this aspect should be deemed final.

# DISCIPLINARY POLICY

YOTTABYTE expects the highest standards of behavior and professionalism from its employees and consultants. Formal disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal, may be taken against any employee where an offence is committed that justifies this. Each offence will be considered on its own merits.

#  General Principles

i.  Minor issues of misconduct or unsatisfactory performance will normally be dealt with on an informal basis, outside of the formal disciplinary procedure. In some cases, the line manager may offer extra training, coaching, advice or support.

ii. All informal discussions will be held in private and the line manager will take account of the opinions of the employee as well as any mitigating circumstances that are provided. Any criticism provided by the line manager will always be constructive. The aim of an informal discussion is to achieve and sustain improvement. Where appropriate, the line manager may confirm what is agreed during the discussion in writing.

iii. If it emerges during discussion that the matter is more serious than previously envisaged, the meeting may be adjourned and then reconvened at a later date under the formal disciplinary procedure.

# Disciplinary Case Notification

Disciplinary Inquiry Form must be raised by the concerned line manager to formally report a disciplinary case to HR. The form must contain details of allegation dully filled and signed by the line manager.

# Warning

An Employee may receive verbal or written warning in case his/her performance falls below expectation, disciplinary issues arise or any relevant reason deemed appropriate by the Company. In case a formal written warning is issued by the HR department, he/she may be placed on probation or suspension. In case the Employees' performance is restored, to the Company's satisfaction, the Employee shall be reinstated. Otherwise, the Company may serve notice for termination as required except in the case of misconduct.

# Suspension

i.  Where an Employee is absent from work without leave or commits an offense, which in the opinion of the Company, amounts to serious misconduct or negligence of duty, he/she may be considered as being suspended and any subsequent full or partial re-instatement shall be at the sole discretion of the Company. For the period of suspension, the Employee is not entitled to receive any wages, salaries or other benefits from the Company.

ii. If the line manager believes that following an alleged offence it would be undesirable for an employee to remain at work, e.g. risk to security, safety, revenue, brand damage or illegal activity, the line manager may request HR, as a precautionary measure, to suspend the employee from duty.

iii. Suspension in all cases must be approved by Head of HR and the Director.

Any conduct likely to endanger the lives or safety of others or the Company\'s property and data or the efficiency and progress of a task or operation may also rank as serious misconduct rendering the Employee concerned liable to dismissal in accordance with Company\'s Policy. No Notice/Salary will be payable in case of Employees are terminated due to misconduct.

Some examples of serious misconduct are as follow. These examples are provided for guidance only and should not be seen as exhaustive.

-   Gross negligence of duty

-   Submission of False information/documentation to the company

-   Refusal to obey orders and instructions

-   Submitting forged documents/ wrong information to the Company

-   Not following regulations as mentioned in the appointment letter and/ or NDA

-   Remaining absent from work without approval or prior notification

-   Stealing or fraud

-   Possession, supply or use of illicit drugs; or being under the influence of intoxicants or drugs on the premises at any time

-   Corruption or attempting to corrupt other Employees

-   Maliciously Damaging Company\'s Property

-   Discriminatory treatment based on race, creed, sex and/or social status

-   Involved in or guilty of any forms of harassment as defined by the laws of Pakistan

# ANTI-HARRASMENT POLICY

YOTTABYTE is devoted to provide and maintain a safe and positive working environment whereby all employees are to be treated with utmost respect and dignity. The Company maintains a "Zero Tolerance" policy with respect to harassment, including, but not limited to, harassment on the basis of gender, age, race, color, religion, sect, disability, marital status and personal characteristics.

# Types of Harassment

Harassment is any objectionable behavior, whether physical, verbal and/or non-verbal, that generally involves a course of inappropriate conduct directed towards any individual. This includes bullying which takes different forms such as humiliation, sarcasm and other acts such as use of demeaning language, coercion, isolation, constant unconstructive criticism, unreasonable refusal of requests for leaves, training or other workplace benefits, offensive jokes and/or personal critical remarks.

Another serious form of Harassment is Sexual Harassment is a serious form of harassment and a major offence. Unwelcome sexual advances, request for sexual favors or other verbal, written objectionable communication, physical advances of sexual nature and/or sexually demeaning attitudes, causing interference with work performance or creating an intimidating, humiliating, hostile and/or offensive work environment.

Abuse is a form of harassment where an employee misuses his/her power, authority and/or position/job- role a) to endanger another employees job, belittle an individual for performance of that job b) to endanger Company's profile, project and/or repute and/or c) to ask for inappropriate favors in order for that individual to keep his job or retain benefits that come along with it, including but not limited to, a wage increase, promotion, training opportunity and/or a transfer and shall include exploitative approach by Resources.

# Rights & Responsibilities

All employees and management of the Company are responsible for maintaining and promoting an environment which is free of harassment and discrimination and for promptly reporting any incidents which they believe to be acts of discrimination or harassment. A person who knows of, or is in possession of information about harassment or discrimination contrary to this policy and who has the authority to penalize or prevent the conduct and fails to exercise that authority may be held accountable for failing to exercise that authority and may be found to have violated this policy.

The Company's Human Resource (HR) Department and management is responsible for effectiveness of this policy in letter and spirit to ensure that:

-   Filing or responding to a bona fide complaint;

# Complainants

Complainants have the right to make a complaint and obtain a review of the same. They are entitled to be accompanied by someone of their choosing during the interview if they choose to do so, and shall not be subject to retaliation for the reason of having made a complaint under this policy. Moreover, complainants need to show their disapproval to such attempts of harassment immediately.

# Accused

The accused has the right to be informed that a complaint has been filed against them, and they shall be presented with a statement of allegations leveled against them and given fair and equal opportunity to shed light on the matter and provide an appropriate response. They also have the right to be accompanied by a person of their choosing to the interview, if they so wish. Moreover, they must co-operate with all those dealing with the investigation of the complaint and maintain confidentiality.

# Witnesses

Witnesses have the right not to be subjected to retaliation or any other kind of pressure in case of their willingness to participate as a witness; and they must also maintain confidentiality and co-operate with all those leading the investigation.

# Inquiry Committee

The Inquiry Committee must ensure all complaints are resolved and parties are informed of the outcome of their decision in a timely manner. They must exercise reasonable care and inform the parties of their rights and responsibilities, collect all crucial evidence, use mediation to resolve a complaint if feasible. A time frame should be agreed upon for completion of investigation, review findings and determine the outcome so that appropriate action can be taken if deemed necessary.

# Procedure

## Informal Procedure of Complaint:

Complaint can be lodged informally i.e. verbally and/ or in writing to respective manager/supervisor, the HR Department Head and/or a member of inquiry committee. An informal approach to resolve a complaint of harassment maybe through mediation between the parties involved and by providing advice and counseling on a strictly confidential basis.

## Formal Procedure of Complaint:

When it is deemed appropriate the Complainant may choose to file a formal complaint. The formal complaint must be in writing and signed by the complainant, it should give an accurate description of the incidents of harassment including the time, place and parties involved. The Committee after receipt of written complaint shall communicate in writing the charges and statement of allegations leveled against the accused. The Committee may enquire into the charge and may examine such oral or documentary evidence in support of the charge or in defense of the accused as it may seem necessary and each party shall be entitled to cross-examine the witnesses against the accused.

Furthermore, if the inquiry committee finds that the complaint involves falsehood or malicious intent or is otherwise made in bad faith, as determined by the investigation, the complainant shall be subject to appropriate disciplinary action as the Committee deems fit.

# Penalties/ Sanctions and Remedies

## Minor Penalties:

-   Issuance of written warning

-   Withholding for a specific period a benefit such as promotion or increment

-   Recovery of compensation payable to complainant, from pay or any other source of the accused

## Major Penalties:

-   Demotion

-   Compulsory Retirement

-   Dismissal from service

-   Monetary penalty/ Financial sanction

## Alternate Legal Remedies:

Nothing in this policy may prevent the complainant or the accused from pursuing formal legal remedies or resolution as provided for under the laws of Pakistan.

# Official Travel

Employees may be required to travel within and outside Pakistan from time to time for various official matters which may include the following:

-   Management Visits

-   Official meetings, events, conferences and seminars

-   Project assignments

All international travelling requests for business purpose will be subject to the approval of the CEO and processed based on availability of budgets according to the standard process. TA/DA will be given to travelling employees' bank account in advance for the number of days employee is travelling. It will be the responsibility of employee to arrange for any specific requirements to carry other currency. In case number of travel days is reduced, employee will be required to return the amount to the company.

The TA/DA for the domestic travel is Rs. 1,500 per day while for the international travel it will be USD 50 per day.

Employees will make necessary arrangements for Visa and Ticket themselves and can get the reimbursement of the same from The Company.
