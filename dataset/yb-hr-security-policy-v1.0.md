## 1. PURPOSE

The purpose of this policy is to ensure that all information security controls and risks related to human resources are implemented, monitored, and reviewed. These controls apply during pre-employment, during employment, and at the time of termination or role change.

---

## 2. SCOPE

This policy applies to all onshore employees, remote employees, and consultants directly employed by YOTTABYTE.

---

## 3. POLICY STATEMENTS

### 3.1 Prior to Employment

- Proper documentation must be maintained during the hiring process, including:
  - Skills
  - Educational background
  - Work experience
  - Defined roles and responsibilities  

---

### 3.1.1 Screening

- Background verification checks must be conducted for all shortlisted/finalized candidates and permanent contractors.
- Checks must comply with applicable laws, regulations, and ethical standards.
- Verification must include:
  - Identity checks (e.g., passport, bank account)
  - Reference checks
  - Academic/professional qualification verification
  - Criminal record checks (for senior positions)  
- All collected information must:
  - Be securely stored
  - Be accessible only on a need-to-know basis  

---

### 3.1.2 Terms and Conditions of Employment

- All employees and contractors must sign employment terms including:
  - Legal and operational responsibilities
  - Duration of responsibilities
  - Confidentiality clauses
  - Disciplinary actions for violations  
- Employees must sign confidentiality agreements covering:
  - During employment
  - After employment  
- HR must:
  - Maintain signed records
  - Manage onboarding documentation  

---

### 3.2 During Employment

All employees and contractors must be aware of and fulfill their information security responsibilities.

---

### 3.2.1 Management Responsibilities

ISF shall:

- Ensure employees understand their security roles before access is granted  
- Promote adherence to information security policies  
- Provide reporting channels for violations  

---

### 3.2.2 Information Security Awareness, Education and Training

- Awareness programs must be implemented and customized  
- Employees must be trained based on their roles  
- Technical staff must receive specialized training  
- Training effectiveness must be evaluated  
- Training records must be maintained  
- Training material must be updated when policies change  

---

### 3.2.3 Disciplinary Process

- A formal disciplinary procedure must exist for policy violations and data breaches  
- Incident Management Team (IMT) responsibilities include:
  - Assessing severity of breaches  
  - Investigating incidents  
  - Coordinating with HR and ISO for disciplinary actions  
  - Removing access privileges when required  
  - Notifying ISO  
  - Providing evidence of incidents  
- IMT reports findings to ISF on a quarterly basis  

---

### 3.3 Termination and Change of Employment

- Managers must notify HR immediately upon resignation or termination  
- HR must initiate an offboarding checklist  
- Access rights must be updated or revoked accordingly  
- Role changes require access review  
- Upon termination:
  - All system access must be removed  
  - All company assets must be returned  
- HR/Managers ensure assets are returned to IT  

---

## 4. ANNUAL REVIEW

- ISMS documents must be reviewed annually or upon updates  
- Internal audits must be conducted annually  
- Reports must be submitted to ISF  
- Processes must be reviewed regularly for compliance
